---
title: "Qualify: Build and track your customer business case"
description: "Use Qualify to evaluate the business opportunity — from key drivers and desired outcomes to ROI calculations, solution fit, and relevant market context."
---

Qualify is a structured workspace for building a complete picture of the business case behind each customer project. Across seven tabs, you can capture the drivers motivating the engagement, the outcomes the customer expects, the financial value at stake, and how well your solution fits their needs. Everything you need to qualify and articulate the opportunity lives here, organized by project.

<Note>
  Like Discovery, Qualify is scoped to a project. Select a project from the dropdown at the top of the page before filling in any tab.
</Note>

## Navigating Qualify

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a project">
    Open **Qualify** from the sidebar and choose a project from the **Project** dropdown. The customer's industry is shown as a badge beneath the selector when available.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Work through the tabs">
    Use the tab bar to move between the seven qualification dimensions. You can fill them in any order — each tab saves independently.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tabs

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Value Drivers">
    The **Value Drivers** tab captures the business motivations behind the project — why the customer is investing and what is driving them to act now. Drivers are organized by category (such as growth, efficiency, risk, or compliance) and can be tagged, assigned, and prioritized.

    You can select from a library of pre-built driver templates or create custom drivers specific to the project.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Market Trends">
    The **Market Trends** tab lets you document relevant industry and market context that supports the business case. Use this tab to record the external forces — competitive pressure, regulatory changes, or technology shifts — that make the timing of this project important.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Outcomes">
    The **Outcomes** tab is where you record the specific business outcomes the customer expects to achieve. Link each outcome to one or more value drivers to show the connection between motivation and result.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Goals & Challenges">
    The **Goals & Challenges** tab lets you document measurable goals tied to the desired outcomes, alongside the challenges standing in the way. Use it to translate broad outcomes into concrete, trackable targets.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Technical Vision">
    The **Technical Vision** tab captures technical requirements, architecture considerations, and any integration or infrastructure context that shapes how the solution will be delivered.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Value">
    The **Value** tab is where you track return on investment and the financial value of the engagement. Record cost savings, revenue impact, efficiency gains, and any other quantifiable benefits the customer expects to realize.

    <Tip>
      The Value tab is the single place for all financial tracking in a project. If you previously used a separate ROI page, that data now lives here.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Solution Fit">
    The **Solution Fit** tab maps your solution's capabilities to the customer's specific needs. Use it to document how each part of your offering addresses the drivers, outcomes, and goals you have captured in the other tabs.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Reference

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Drivers: templates and custom drivers">
    The Value Drivers tab supports two types of drivers:

    - **Driver templates** — reusable, organization-level drivers that apply across multiple projects. Create these once and select them for any project.
    - **Custom drivers** — project-specific drivers created directly in the Qualify tab. Use these for motivations that are unique to a single customer or engagement.

    Both types can be assigned to team members and tagged for filtering.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Linking drivers to outcomes">
    After adding drivers on the Value Drivers tab, switch to the Outcomes tab to link each outcome back to one or more drivers. This creates a traceable chain from business motivation through to the expected result, which is useful when building the business case with the customer.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Using qualification data in other modules">
    The goals, drivers, and outcomes you capture in Qualify are visible when building your Customer Success Plan. Referencing them in your plan keeps the engagement grounded in the business case you qualified.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  Switching projects clears the tab view and loads qualification data for the newly selected project. Make sure you are on the right project before editing.
</Warning>
