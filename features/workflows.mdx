---
title: "Workflows: Map customer business processes visually"
description: "Build visual flowcharts and process diagrams to document a customer's current or future-state business processes, organized by project and department."
---

Workflows gives you a visual canvas for mapping out how your customers' business processes work. You can document the current state of a process, design a future-state flow, or map an onboarding sequence — all as a drag-and-drop diagram linked to a specific project and department.

Each workflow lives inside a project and is scoped to a department, keeping your process maps organized across the customer portfolio.

## Key concepts

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Workflow" icon="sitemap">
    A named process diagram associated with a project and department. You can have multiple workflows per project, one for each process you want to map.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Steps (nodes)" icon="circle">
    The individual steps in a process. You drag and drop nodes onto the canvas and position them to reflect the sequence of the workflow.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Edges" icon="arrow-right">
    Connections between steps. Draw an edge from one step to another to show the flow between them. Edges can have optional labels.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Department" icon="building">
    Every workflow belongs to a department. Select the department when you create the workflow to keep diagrams organized.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Departments

When you create a workflow, you assign it to one of the following departments:

- Operations
- Finance
- Sales
- Marketing
- Engineering
- HR
- IT
- Customer Service
- Other

## Create a workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a project">
    On the Workflows page, choose the project you want to map a process for from the project selector.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new workflow">
    Click **New Workflow**. Enter a name, an optional description, and select the department this process belongs to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the diagram">
    The workflow canvas opens automatically. Drag steps onto the canvas and connect them with edges to build the process diagram. Reposition nodes by dragging them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the canvas">
    Click **Save** to persist your diagram. Your step layout and connections are saved with the workflow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Give each workflow a clear name that describes the process it represents, such as "Invoice Approval Process" or "Customer Onboarding Flow". This makes it easier to find the right diagram when a project has multiple workflows.
</Tip>

## Use cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Current-state process documentation">
    Map how a customer's team currently handles a process before any changes. Use this as a baseline for measuring improvement or identifying bottlenecks.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Future-state redesign">
    Design the improved or automated version of a process. Share the diagram with stakeholders to align on what the new workflow will look like.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Onboarding flow mapping">
    Document the steps a customer needs to complete during onboarding — integrations, training, configuration, go-live — so everyone sees the full picture.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
