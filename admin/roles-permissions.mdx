---
title: "Roles and permissions: Control what teams can do"
description: "Understand the three base roles in Bluplai, create custom roles, and control which modules and sections each team member can access."
---

Bluplai uses a two-layer access model. First, every team member holds a **role** that defines their overall capability level. Second, admins can fine-tune access further by toggling individual **module permissions** per user. Together, roles and module permissions give you precise control over what each person on your team can see and do.

<Note>
  The **Admin** page is only accessible to users with the Admin role. Members with Editor or Viewer roles will not see it in the sidebar.
</Note>

## Base roles

Bluplai has three built-in roles that always exist. Every team member is assigned one of these, either directly or through a custom role that inherits from one of them.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Admin" icon="shield">
    Full access to all features, modules, and settings. Admins can invite and remove team members, change roles, manage module permissions, and configure organization settings.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Editor" icon="pencil">
    Can view and edit content across the modules they have been granted access to. Editors cannot manage team members, roles, or organization settings.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Viewer" icon="eye">
    Read-only access to the modules they have been granted access to. Viewers cannot create or edit records.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Custom roles

In addition to the three base roles, admins can create **custom roles** — named presets that inherit from a base role and carry a default set of module permissions. When you assign a custom role to a new member, their module access is seeded automatically from the preset, saving you from configuring each module one by one.

Common examples: "CSM", "Sales Lead", "Executive Sponsor".

### Creating a custom role

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Admin → Roles">
    Navigate to **Admin** in the sidebar and click the **Roles** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New role">
    Click the **New role** button in the Custom roles section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the role">
    Give the role a name and description, choose which base role it inherits from, and set its default module and section permissions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The new custom role is now available in the invite dialog and the role selector on the Users tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Custom role permissions are applied when the role is first assigned to a user. Changing the custom role definition later does not automatically update existing users — you would need to reassign them to the updated role.
</Tip>

## Changing a member's role

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Admin → Users">
    Navigate to **Admin** and open the **Users** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the member">
    Locate the team member whose role you want to change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the role">
    Use the role dropdown next to the member's name to select a new base role or custom role. The change takes effect immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Changing a member's role from Admin to Editor or Viewer immediately removes their access to the Admin page and all admin-only features.
</Warning>

## Module permissions

Module permissions let you grant or revoke a team member's access to specific modules — independently of their role. For example, you may have an Editor who should only work in the **Plan** and **Engage** modules, with no access to **Qualify** or **Discover**.

### Managing module permissions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Admin → Permissions">
    Navigate to **Admin** and click the **Permissions** tab. The tab shows a matrix of team members and modules.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the member">
    Use the search bar or company filter to locate the member whose permissions you want to adjust.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle module access">
    Click the toggle switch in the column for each module to grant or revoke access. Changes are saved immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Admin users always have access to all modules regardless of what the Permissions tab shows. Module permission toggles apply to Editor and Viewer role members only.
</Note>

### Section-level permissions

Within each module, you can also control access to individual sections. For example, within the **Home** module you can enable or disable **Analytics**, **Activity Feed**, or **My Tasks** independently. Section toggles appear when you expand a module's permissions for a specific user.

See [Bluplai modules and feature access](/admin/modules) for a full list of modules and their sections.
