Stakeholders are the people at your customer accounts who matter to the success of your engagements. A stakeholder might be an executive sponsor, a day-to-day champion, a technical decision-maker, or a skeptic you need to bring onside. Tracking stakeholders in Bluplai helps you stay on top of who you need to reach, how well those relationships are progressing, and whether the right people are engaged at each stage of the customer journey.

## What a stakeholder record contains

Each stakeholder is associated with a customer account (not a specific project). This means the same person shows up across all projects for that account, which reflects how real relationships work.

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Name** | The person's full name. Required. |
| **Role / Title** | Their job title or role at the company. |
| **Department** | The team or department they belong to. |
| **Email** | Contact email address. |
| **Phone** | Contact phone number. |
| **Influence level** | How much sway this person has: High, Medium, or Low. |
| **Engagement status** | Your assessment of where they stand: Champion, Supporter, Neutral, Skeptic, or Blocker. |
| **Notes** | Free-text notes about the person or the relationship. |

## How to add a stakeholder

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Stakeholders">
    Click **Stakeholders** in the left sidebar to open the stakeholders list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Stakeholder">
    Click the **Add Stakeholder** button. A dialog opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the customer account">
    Choose which account this person belongs to. This is required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the stakeholder's name">
    Type their full name. This is the only other required field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in contact and relationship details">
    Add their role, department, email, phone, influence level, and engagement status. You can update any of these later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Add Stakeholder** to save the record. The person now appears in the stakeholder list and in the relevant account's Stakeholders tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Stakeholder views

The Stakeholders page offers five views. Switch between them using the view pill controls at the top of the page.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Contacts">
    The default view. Shows all stakeholders as cards with their name, title, company, contact details, influence level, and engagement status. Use this view for day-to-day reference and to edit individual records.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Relationship">
    Shows stakeholders grouped by their engagement status — Champions, Supporters, and Blockers. Use this view to quickly assess the overall relationship landscape for a given account or across your portfolio.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Engagement">
    Displays a heatmap of monthly interaction frequency across stakeholders. Use this to spot stakeholders you haven't engaged with recently.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Influence">
    A visual map that groups stakeholders by influence level. High-influence people appear larger. Use this to identify where your most important relationships stand and who needs more attention.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Health">
    Shows relationship health indicators for each stakeholder — Healthy, At risk, or Critical. Use this view when preparing for QBRs or planning outreach campaigns.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Why stakeholder tracking matters

Customer success depends on reaching the right people at the right time. Keeping your stakeholder records current helps you:

- **Maintain executive alignment** — know who the senior sponsor is and whether they are engaged, so you can escalate or re-engage before issues become risks.
- **Multi-thread the relationship** — avoid single-threaded accounts where one departure puts the entire engagement at risk. Track multiple champions and supporters across departments.
- **Prepare for renewals and expansions** — use the influence and engagement maps to identify who needs to be brought in for the commercial conversation at the Extend stage.
- **Spot emerging blockers early** — if a stakeholder's engagement status shifts to Skeptic or Blocker, you have a clear signal to act before it affects the project.

<Tip>
  Review your stakeholder maps before any major customer milestone — kickoffs, QBRs, renewals, or executive briefings. A few minutes of review can surface relationship gaps you might otherwise miss.
</Tip>

## Filtering stakeholders

On the Stakeholders list you can:

- **Search** by name, role, or account name.
- **Filter by account** to view only the stakeholders for a specific customer.

## Engagement status options

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Champion** | Actively advocates for you and your solution within the company. |
| **Supporter** | Broadly positive and cooperative, though not actively championing you. |
| **Neutral** | Has not formed a strong view either way. |
| **Skeptic** | Has doubts or concerns about the engagement or solution. Needs attention. |
| **Blocker** | Actively working against the engagement. Requires a deliberate plan to address. |

<Note>
  Stakeholders are associated with an account, not a project. If a customer has multiple projects in Bluplai, the same stakeholders appear on each project — because your relationship with those people spans the whole account, not just one initiative.
</Note>
