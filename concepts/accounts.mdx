An account in Bluplai represents a customer company you manage. Every account is the central record that ties together projects, stakeholders, engagement data, and health metrics for that customer. You navigate to **Accounts** from the sidebar to see your full portfolio at a glance, search and filter it, and jump into any individual account to work on it.

## What an account contains

Each account holds the following information:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Name** | The company name. Required. |
| **Industry** | The sector the company operates in (e.g. Technology, Healthcare, Finance). |
| **Website** | The company's URL. |
| **Location** | City or region (e.g. "New York, USA"). |
| **Description** | A brief note about the company or the relationship. |
| **Account status** | Whether the account is Active, Pending, or Inactive. |
| **Lifecycle stage** | Where the customer sits in Bluplai's 8-stage lifecycle model. |
| **Health score** | A number from 0 to 100 representing the account's overall health. |

## Account status

Every account carries one of three status values. Use these to reflect the current state of your relationship with the customer.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Active">
    The account is a current customer with an ongoing engagement. Active accounts appear in your default view and count toward your active portfolio.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Pending">
    The account is in progress but not yet fully onboarded or confirmed. Use Pending for prospects who are close to signing, or customers who are partway through an onboarding that hasn't been completed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Inactive">
    The account is no longer an active engagement. Inactive accounts remain in your records but are visually separated so they don't clutter your working view.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

You can change the status directly from the account card on the Accounts list, or by editing the account.

## Health score

The health score is a number between 0 and 100 that gives a quick signal about how well the account is doing. You set this value manually — Bluplai does not calculate it automatically. Use it to reflect your own assessment of the account's health based on engagement, adoption, satisfaction, or any criteria your team tracks.

| Range | Status |
|---|---|
| 80 – 100 | Healthy |
| 60 – 79 | At risk |
| 0 – 59 | Critical |

<Tip>
  Update the health score regularly — before QBRs, after renewal calls, or any time something significant changes in the relationship. Consistent scoring makes your portfolio-level analytics more meaningful.
</Tip>

## How to create an account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Accounts">
    Click **Accounts** in the left sidebar to open the accounts list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Customer">
    Click the **Add Customer** button in the top-right corner of the page. A dialog opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the company name">
    Type the company name. This is the only required field. Everything else is optional.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in additional details (optional)">
    Add the industry, website, location, and a short description if you have them. You can always edit these later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Customer">
    Click **Create Customer** to save the account. It appears immediately in your accounts list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After creating an account, open it and use the edit dialog to set the lifecycle stage and health score.

## Filtering and searching accounts

The Accounts page gives you several ways to narrow down the list:

- **Search bar** — filters by company name or industry as you type.
- **Status cards** — click the Total, Active, Pending, or Inactive card at the top to filter by status.
- **Filters panel** — use the filter controls to narrow by industry or lifecycle stage.

<Tip>
  Combine the lifecycle stage filter with the status filter to focus on a specific cohort — for example, all Active accounts at the Onboard stage.
</Tip>

## What links to an account

Each account is the parent record for everything else you do in Bluplai for that customer. From the account detail view you can access:

- **Projects** — all engagements and initiatives linked to this account, with status and progress.
- **Stakeholders** — the people at the customer company who matter to the engagement.
- **Discovery** — goals, challenges, and the customer's desired future state.
- **Pulse** — interviews, surveys, and satisfaction scores linked to this account.
- **Adoption** — product adoption metrics tracked per module.
- **Events** — customer-facing events associated with this account.

See [Projects](/concepts/projects), [Stakeholders](/concepts/stakeholders), and [Discovery](/features/discovery) for more detail on each area.

## Deleting an account

<Warning>
  Deleting an account permanently removes it along with all associated projects, stakeholders, ROI calculations, and other related data. This action cannot be undone. If you want to preserve the data but stop working on the account, set the status to **Inactive** instead.
</Warning>

To delete an account, open the edit dialog from the account card or account detail page and click **Delete**.
