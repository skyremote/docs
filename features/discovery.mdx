---
title: "Discovery: Capture customer context and goals"
description: "Use Discovery to build a structured picture of each customer — their goals, challenges, and current state — before you start delivering value."
---

Discovery gives you a dedicated workspace to capture everything you learn about a customer during the early stages of an engagement. Rather than scattering notes across emails and documents, you can organize all of your research — from high-level account facts to workshop outputs — in one place, scoped to each of your projects.

<Note>
  Discovery data is per-project. Use the project dropdown at the top of the page to switch between projects and view or edit that project's discovery records.
</Note>

## Getting started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a project">
    Open **Discovery** from the sidebar. Use the **Select Project** dropdown to choose the project you want to work on. The page will load that project's discovery sections automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in each section">
    Work through the five sections below. Each section is collapsible — click the section header to expand or collapse it so you can focus on one area at a time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your work">
    Each section saves as you type or when you submit a form. There is no separate save button — your changes are persisted in real time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Discovery sections

Discovery is organized into five sections, each covering a distinct part of the customer picture.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Snapshot — Current state of the organization">
    The Snapshot section gives you a high-level overview of the customer account. Use it to record key facts about the organization — such as company size, industry, key contacts, and any important context that frames the rest of the engagement.

    You can add multiple snapshot cards, each covering a different theme or area of the account. Cards support rich text so you can include formatted notes, bullet lists, or structured information.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="2. Goals — Desired state with value logic">
    The Goals section is where you capture the customer's strategic objectives and the outcomes they want to achieve. Document what success looks like from the customer's perspective and how their goals connect to measurable value.

    Use this section to align your team on what you are ultimately trying to help the customer accomplish.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="3. Challenges — Friction points mapped as opportunities">
    The Challenges section records the pain points, blockers, and current-state problems the customer is facing. Framing challenges as opportunities helps you stay solution-focused and gives your team a clear picture of where the customer needs the most help.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4. Planning Board — Thinking and strategy layer">
    The Planning Board is a free-form collaborative space for notes, strategic thinking, and early-stage planning. Use it as a shared scratchpad to explore ideas, map out approaches, or capture anything that does not fit neatly into the other sections.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="5. Workshop — Alignment and structured execution">
    The Workshop section holds structured notes and outputs from discovery workshops and alignment sessions. Use it to document what was discussed, decisions that were made, and next steps that came out of working sessions with the customer.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tips for effective discovery

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Start with Snapshot" icon="camera">
    Fill in the Snapshot first. It gives everyone on your team the essential account context before diving into goals and challenges.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use Planning Board for drafts" icon="pencil">
    Use the Planning Board to think through your approach before committing ideas to the more structured sections.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Capture challenges as opportunities" icon="lightbulb">
    When recording challenges, note how each one represents an opportunity for your solution to deliver value.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Document workshop outputs promptly" icon="clock">
    Add workshop notes right after a session while the details are fresh. Link outcomes back to the Goals section.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Each section is collapsible. Click any section header to expand or collapse it. Use the browser's back button to return to the previous section after navigating away.
</Tip>
