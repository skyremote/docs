The Activity log is a chronological record of every change made in your organization. Any time a team member creates or updates a customer, project, initiative, or stakeholder, an entry appears here. You can use the Activity log to stay current on what your colleagues are working on, review what changed in an account before a call, or audit a specific update after the fact.

To open the Activity log, click **Activity** in the sidebar, or click **View all** on the Dashboard's recent activity widget.

## What each entry shows

Every activity entry contains the following information:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Entity type | The type of record that changed: customer, project, initiative, stakeholder, or member |
| Entity name | The name of the specific record that was created or updated |
| Action | What happened: **created**, **updated**, or **deleted** |
| Field changed | For updates, the specific field that changed—for example, `status`, `health_score`, `lifecycle_stage`, `ARR`, or `priority` |
| Timestamp | When the change happened, shown as a relative time (e.g., "2h ago") or a date for older entries |

## Activity types tracked

Bluplai tracks three action types across all entity types:

| Action | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Created** | A new record was added — a customer was onboarded, a project was created, an initiative was planned, or a stakeholder was mapped. |
| **Updated** | An existing record was changed. The entry shows which field changed — for example, a project's lifecycle stage moved from Onboard to Use, or a health score was revised. |
| **Deleted** | A record was removed. Deleted entries are shown in the log but are not clickable since the record no longer exists. |

## Navigating from an entry

Click any activity entry to navigate directly to the affected record. For example, clicking an update to a project's status takes you straight to that project's detail page. Entries for deleted records are not clickable.

<Tip>
  Use the Dashboard's date range filter to focus the recent activity widget on a specific time window—such as the past week or a particular quarter—before clicking "View all" to open a scoped Activity log view.
</Tip>

## Scope and visibility

Activity is scoped to your organization. You see all changes made by every member of your team—not just your own. This makes the Activity log a useful tool for understanding what your colleagues are working on and coordinating across accounts without stepping on each other's work.

<Note>
  The Activity log shows the 50 most recent entries. If you need to investigate changes further back in time, use the date range filter on the Dashboard to focus the recent activity widget on the period you're interested in.
</Note>
