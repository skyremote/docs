Demo mode lets you walk through every part of Bluplai using realistic sample data, without needing to connect your own customers first. It's a good way to understand how the platform works before onboarding your team or migrating existing accounts.

## What's included in demo mode

When you sign in with a demo account, you get a fully pre-loaded workspace that includes:

- **Customers** — five sample accounts across industries like Technology, Healthcare, and Manufacturing, each with health scores, ARR, lifecycle stages, and locations
- **Stakeholders** — contacts mapped to each account with roles, influence levels, and engagement scores
- **Projects** — active projects with milestones, actions, and deliverables
- **Initiatives** — success plan initiatives with assigned owners and due dates
- **ROI data** — calculated return on investment figures tied to sample projects
- **Surveys** — pre-built surveys with sample responses and diagnostic scores
- **Workflows** — workflow diagrams showing customer process maps
- **Events** — logged customer events and activity history
- **Analytics** — dashboard stats, activity trends, module completion rates, and engagement data
- **Assessments** — digital maturity assessments with scores per category
- **Audit log** — a history of activity entries across the demo workspace

## How to access demo mode

Sign in at your Bluplai URL using the demo credentials your team has been provided. Bluplai automatically detects the demo account and loads the sample workspace in place of live data. You don't need to configure anything — the demo data is ready immediately.

## What you can and can't do in demo mode

Demo mode lets you navigate and interact with every feature of Bluplai. You can open accounts, browse projects, view stakeholder maps, read survey results, and explore the analytics dashboard exactly as you would in a live workspace.

<Warning>
  Changes you make in demo mode are not saved. Any edits, new records, or updates you attempt will show a "Demo mode" notification and will not be persisted. This is expected — demo mode is read-only to protect the shared sample dataset.
</Warning>

Actions that trigger a "Demo mode" notification include creating or editing accounts, adding stakeholders, updating project details, sending surveys, and modifying success plans. You can still click through all flows and see how the forms and editors work — you just won't see the changes reflected after the notification.

## Switching to a real organization

When you're ready to work with real customer data, create a new organization with a standard (non-demo) account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign out of the demo account">
    Click your profile in the sidebar and select **Sign out**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in with your real account">
    Sign in with your work email or Google account. If you don't have an account yet, select **Sign up** on the login page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your organization">
    On first login with a real account, you'll be prompted to name your organization. Enter your company name and confirm to create your workspace.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your real organization starts empty — no demo data carries over. From there, follow the [quickstart guide](/quickstart) to add your first account and invite your team.
