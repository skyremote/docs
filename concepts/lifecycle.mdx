---
title: "Customer lifecycle: The 8 stages in Bluplai"
description: "Bluplai uses an 8-stage customer lifecycle from Discover to Extend. Learn what each stage means and how stages drive your work across accounts and projects."
---

Bluplai organizes the customer journey into eight sequential lifecycle stages. These stages describe where a customer relationship is at any given moment — from the first time you engage with a prospect all the way through to expanding the relationship. Both accounts and projects can be tagged with a lifecycle stage, which lets you filter your portfolio, focus your team, and use the right tools for each phase.

## The 8 lifecycle stages

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Discover">
    You are in early contact with the prospect. The goal at this stage is to understand the customer's world — their business, challenges, and priorities — and to determine whether there is a genuine fit. Use the Discovery module to capture workshop outputs, goals, and initial observations.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Evaluate">
    The prospect is actively assessing whether your solution meets their needs. You are working through qualification, building the business case, and gathering the information needed to make a recommendation. The Qualify module — including ROI and value analysis — is most relevant here.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Consider">
    The prospect is comparing options and weighing the decision. Your focus is on differentiating value, addressing objections, and ensuring the right stakeholders are engaged and aligned. Stakeholder tracking becomes especially important at this stage.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Buy">
    The customer has made the decision to proceed. Commercial and contractual steps are underway. You may be finalizing scope, timelines, and success criteria before formal onboarding begins.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Onboard">
    The customer is now live and being set up. Onboarding activities, initial configuration, and early training are the focus. Use the Customer Success Plan (CSP) to structure the onboarding journey with milestones and deliverables.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use">
    The customer is actively using the product. Your focus shifts to ensuring they are getting value from their existing use cases and that adoption is progressing as planned. Track usage and engagement metrics to identify risks early.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Adopt">
    The customer has embedded the product into their core workflows. They are realizing the value promised during the sales process. This is the stage where you validate ROI, run satisfaction surveys, and build the relationship for the long term.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Extend">
    The customer is ready to grow the relationship — through renewal, expansion, or new use cases. Use engagement data and the stakeholder map to identify expansion opportunities and prepare for the commercial conversation.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How lifecycle stages work

You can assign a lifecycle stage to both **accounts** and **projects** independently.

- Setting a lifecycle stage on an **account** reflects the overall state of your relationship with that customer company.
- Setting a lifecycle stage on a **project** reflects where that specific engagement is within the lifecycle, which may differ from the account-level stage if you run multiple projects with the same customer.

<Note>
  A single account can have multiple projects at different lifecycle stages. For example, one project may be in Adopt while another with the same customer starts at Discover for a new use case.
</Note>

## How lifecycle stage drives your work

Each stage maps to specific tools and modules in Bluplai:

| Stage | Primary tools |
|---|---|
| Discover | Discovery module — goals, challenges, snapshot cards, workshop notes |
| Evaluate | Qualify module — business drivers, outcomes, ROI, value analysis |
| Consider | Stakeholder map — influence, engagement, relationship health |
| Buy | Account plan, success criteria documentation |
| Onboard | Customer Success Plan (CSP) — initiatives, actions, milestones |
| Use | Adoption tracking, engagement metrics |
| Adopt | Satisfaction surveys, ROI validation, health scoring |
| Extend | Stakeholder Opportunity map, expansion planning |

## Using lifecycle stage filters

Both the **Accounts** and **Projects** pages support filtering by lifecycle stage.

<Tip>
  Use the lifecycle stage filter to run stage-specific reviews. For example, filter to **Onboard** each week to check in on all customers in active onboarding, or filter to **Extend** to prepare for upcoming renewal and expansion conversations.
</Tip>
