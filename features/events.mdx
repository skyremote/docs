---
title: "Events: Track customer engagements and registrations"
description: "Create and manage events linked to your customer accounts — from webinars and training sessions to workshops, QBRs, and executive briefings."
---

Events lets you create and track customer-facing engagements directly inside Bluplai. Whether you're running an onboarding workshop, a product training webinar, a quarterly business review, or an executive briefing, you can log the event, link it to a customer account, and track registrations and attendance — all in one place.

## Event types

When creating an event, choose the type that best describes it. Available types include: **Webinar**, **Workshop**, **Training**, **QBR**, **Review**, **Meeting**, **Conference**, **Trade Show**, and **Meetup**. The type is used for filtering and reporting across your events list.

## Create an event

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Events">
    Click **Events** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new event">
    Click **Create Event**. Fill in the event details:

    | Field | Description |
    |-------|-------------|
    | **Title** | The name of the event (required) |
    | **Description** | Optional details about the event agenda or purpose |
    | **Start date** | The date the event begins (required) |
    | **End date** | Optional end date for multi-day events |
    | **Type** | The event category (webinar, workshop, training, QBR, etc.) |
    | **Location** | Physical address, city, or "Online" |
    | **Expected attendees** | Anticipated headcount (e.g., "50") |
    | **Registration URL** | Optional external registration link |
    | **Featured** | Toggle on to highlight the event in the featured list |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the event">
    Click **Create Event**. The event appears in the events list immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Manage registrations and attendance

Once an event exists, open its detail page to manage who is registered and who attended.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the event">
    Click an event from the events list to open its detail page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add registrations">
    Use the registrations section to add the names of customers or contacts who have registered for the event.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track attendance">
    After the event, update attendance records to reflect who actually showed up. The event detail page shows both registrations and attendees side by side.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Events are linked to customer accounts so you can see all events associated with a specific customer from their account page. This gives you a complete engagement history without needing to search across the events list.
</Note>

## Use cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Onboarding workshops">
    Create a workshop event at the start of an implementation. Track which customer contacts attended so you have a record of who completed onboarding training.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Product training">
    Log recurring training sessions per customer and track attendance to see which users have been trained on specific features.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Quarterly business reviews (QBRs)">
    Record QBRs as events linked to the customer account. The event history becomes part of the engagement timeline alongside your projects and success plans.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Executive briefings">
    Track strategic sessions with executive sponsors. Note expected attendees and link the event to the account for visibility across your team.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Events dashboard

The top of the Events page shows a summary of:

- **Total events** — all events in your organization
- **Upcoming** — events with a future start date or upcoming status
- **Featured** — events you have marked as featured
- **Registrations** — total registrations across all events
